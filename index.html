Professional Summary

I am a pre-business student at Brigham Young University preparing to apply to the Strategic Management and Information Systems programs. I am especially interested in how organizations can use data, systems, and thoughtful processes to make better decisions and operate more efficiently.

Through coursework, I have gained experience with SQL, Tableau, Excel, and business fundamentals. I enjoy cleaning messy data, spotting patterns, and presenting insights clearly. I am motivated by roles that blend technical problem solving with collaboration and team work.

Before BYU, I ran a small landscaping business, where I managed clients, scheduling, and finances, and learned firsthand how to design processes that support accuracy and reliability. My mission experience in Madagascar deepened my interest in using data to solve real problems.

I value continuous learning, clear communication, and hard work. I am excited to build a career where I can grow technically and contribute to meaningful projects.

Data & Decision-Making

While serving in Madagascar, I spent a significant portion of my time in the mission office, where I was responsible for gathering, cleaning, and organizing weekly, monthly, and yearly statistics. I reported on key metrics such as teaching, outreach, and progress toward goals.

That experience showed me how powerful numbers can be when they are tracked consistently and presented clearly. I learned to spot patterns in the data, ask questions, and communicate trends to leaders making decisions.

Working with these reports sparked a desire to study analytics more deeply and use data to support planning, accountability, and improvement.
